Christina's Child Care Center

Logging in and adding a child's daily report

The whole process, start to finish, with pictures of the real screens. It takes under a minute.

For staff Families: see step 4 for what you receive
1

Walk up to the room iPad

Every room has a mounted iPad already on the staff sign-in screen. You do not need your own device. If the screen is asleep, tap it once to wake it.

The iPad is shared by everyone in the room. You sign in for what you do, then the next person signs in for theirs. Nothing is tied to one device.
2

Enter your 4-digit PIN

Tap Quick PIN and enter your personal 4-digit PIN on the keypad. That single action signs you in and clocks you in or out. You do not log your hours anywhere else.

The real sign-in screen. Your PIN is checked securely; your name and role come from your account, not the device.

Wrong PIN? It just says try again. If you forget it, ask a lead or the director to look it up. Never share your PIN.
3

Open Daily Report and log the moment

Once you are in, tap Daily Report in the left menu. Then: pick the child, choose the type (note, meal, nap, diaper, bathroom, activity, medication, photo, or incident), add a short note or a photo, and tap Log it.

The real Daily Report screen. Notice your name at the bottom left: every entry is recorded as you, with the time and room.

Photos: choose Photo, attach the picture, add an optional caption, and Log it. The photo goes straight onto that child's report.
4

Where the information goes

The moment you tap Log it, it flows on its own. You never enter anything twice.

That child's parent sees it on their report The room's daily report The owner's reports view

And the bigger picture: your clock-in and each child's check-in feed payroll, staff-to-child ratios, the budget, and the owner dashboard. Here is the whole map:

The full flow, also at /pathways. Staff time on the left, child attendance on the right, meeting at the live ratio check.

For families: you only ever see your own child. Open Daily Report in your parent dashboard to see naps, meals, notes, and photos as they happen through the day.
5

If a time is wrong

Forgot to clock out, or a child's check-in time is off? It does not have to be perfect in the moment. A manager can correct any clock-in/out or check-in/out from the admin side, and payroll and the room counts update right away.

Tell a lead or the director. Accuracy gets fixed by a quick correction, not by stressing at the iPad.
One PIN to sign in and clock in. One screen to log the moment. Everything else follows.
Times can always be corrected for accuracy
