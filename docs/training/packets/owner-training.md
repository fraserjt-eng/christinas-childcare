## SECTION 2: Financial Dashboard

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Finance > Dashboard

### What You See

The financial dashboard gives you a single-screen view of center finances:

- **Monthly P&L** -- Revenue minus expenses, with net income/loss
- **Budget vs. Actual** -- Side-by-side comparison for the current month
- **Expense Categories** -- Where money is going, broken into:
  - Labor (wages, benefits, taxes)
  - Food and supplies
  - Rent and utilities
  - Insurance
  - Training and professional development
  - Marketing
  - Miscellaneous
- **Revenue Sources** -- Tuition, CACFP reimbursement, state subsidies, grants
- **Year-to-Date Trends** -- Line charts showing revenue and expense patterns over time

### Reading the Budget vs. Actual Report

| Column | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Category | Expense or revenue line item |
| Budgeted | What you planned to spend/earn this month |
| Actual | What you actually spent/earned |
| Variance | Difference (positive = under budget, negative = over) |
| % Variance | How far off from plan |

**Action triggers:**
- Variance over 10% in any category: investigate this week
- Variance over 20%: investigate today
- Three consecutive months of overspending in one category: structural problem, needs a plan

### Monthly Review Habit

On the 1st of every month:
1. Open the financial dashboard
2. Review last month's P&L
3. Check budget vs. actual for every category
4. Flag anything over 10% variance
5. Note trends (is the gap growing or shrinking?)
6. Export the report for your records

## SECTION 3: Revenue Forecasting

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Finance > Forecasting

### Reading the Forecast

The forecast projects revenue for the next 3, 6, and 12 months based on:
- Current enrollment numbers
- Tuition rates
- CACFP reimbursement estimates
- Known upcoming changes (children aging out, new enrollments, rate changes)

### "What-If" Scenario Modeling

This is where the platform saves you the most strategic time. Run scenarios before making decisions:

**Scenario 1: Enrollment Change**
- "What happens if we lose 3 children next month?"
- Adjust the enrollment number, see the revenue impact
- Identify how much you'd need to cut or how fast you'd need to refill

**Scenario 2: Rate Adjustment**
- "What if we raise tuition by $25/week?"
- See the annual revenue impact
- Compare against local market rates shown in the tool

**Scenario 3: Staffing Change**
- "What if we add a part-time assistant?"
- Enter the hourly rate and hours
- See the impact on labor cost percentage and net income

**Scenario 4: CACFP Optimization**
- "What if we improve meal count submission to 100%?"
- See the reimbursement increase from eliminating missed counts

### How to Run a Scenario

1. Open the Forecasting page
2. Tap "New Scenario"
3. Adjust the variable (enrollment, rates, staffing, etc.)
4. The dashboard recalculates revenue, expenses, and net income in real time
5. Save scenarios to compare them side by side
6. Use the comparison view to make your decision

## SECTION 4: Cross-Site Operations

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Cross-Site Dashboard

### Dual-Center Monitoring

You operate two locations (Brooklyn Park and Crystal). The cross-site dashboard shows both centers on one screen:

- **Attendance** -- Real-time check-in counts at each site
- **Ratios** -- Green/yellow/red status for every classroom at both sites
- **Meal Counts** -- Submission status by site
- **Task Completion** -- Percentage of tasks done at each site
- **Staff on Duty** -- Who's working where right now
- **Incidents** -- Any open incidents at either site
- **Financial Summary** -- Revenue and expenses by site

### Management by Exception

You don't need to dig into every detail at both sites every day. The dashboard highlights exceptions:

- **Red alerts** -- Something needs your attention right now (ratio violation, missed meal count deadline, open incident)
- **Yellow flags** -- Something is trending in the wrong direction (rising overtime, declining meal count compliance, stalled enrollment leads)
- **Green** -- Running smoothly. No action needed.

**Daily habit:** Open the cross-site dashboard first thing in the morning. Scan for red and yellow items. Address those. If everything is green, move on to strategic work.

### Site Comparison

Compare performance metrics between sites:
- Which site has better meal count compliance?
- Which site has lower labor cost as a percentage of revenue?
- Which site has higher parent engagement (photo views, message response rates)?

Use these comparisons to identify best practices at one site and transfer them to the other.

## SECTION 5: Payroll Management

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Finance > Payroll

### Weekly Payroll Process

**Every Friday:**
1. Open the Payroll page
2. Review time entries for all employees at both sites
3. Check for:
   - Missing clock-in or clock-out (flagged in yellow)
   - Overtime approaching or exceeded (flagged in red)
   - Schedule discrepancies (worked hours vs. scheduled hours)
4. Approve or correct each entry
5. Generate pay stubs

**Every Monday (or your pay period close):**
1. Final review of approved time entries
2. Generate the payroll summary
3. Export for your payroll processor
4. File the period record

### Time Entry Review Checklist

- [ ] All employees have clock-in and clock-out for every scheduled shift
- [ ] No unexplained gaps (missing punches resolved)
- [ ] Overtime hours flagged and approved or adjusted
- [ ] Break times recorded correctly
- [ ] Any manual time adjustments have a documented reason

### Pay Stub Generation

1. Select the pay period
2. Select employees (all or individual)
3. Tap "Generate Pay Stubs"
4. Review each stub for accuracy
5. Distribute (print or send digitally)

### Overtime Prevention

The payroll dashboard shows a running hours total for each employee during the current week. When someone approaches 35 hours:
- Yellow flag appears
- You can adjust the remaining schedule to prevent overtime
- If overtime is necessary, approve it in advance with a note

**Cost context:** Unplanned overtime at $18/hour adds up fast. Five hours of unexpected OT per week across both sites costs $7,000+ per year.

## SECTION 6: Meeting Efficiency

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Meetings

### Timed Agenda Builder

1. Create a new meeting
2. Add agenda items with time allocations:
   - Topic name
   - Time allotted (in minutes)
   - Discussion lead (who owns this item)
   - Type: Decision, Update, Discussion, or Brainstorm
3. The total time calculates automatically
4. During the meeting, a timer counts down each item

### Running an Efficient Meeting

- Set a hard stop time. Meetings expand to fill available time if you let them.
- Assign a timekeeper (or use the built-in timer)
- "Update" items should be 2-3 minutes max. Send details in advance.
- "Decision" items need a clear outcome: approved, denied, or tabled with a date.
- "Discussion" items have a time limit. If unresolved, schedule a follow-up.

### Action Items to Tasks

This is the connection that makes meetings productive:

1. During the meeting, capture action items as they come up
2. Assign each action item to a person with a due date
3. When you close the meeting, action items automatically appear on assigned employees' task boards
4. Track completion from your dashboard

No more "I thought someone else was doing that." The system holds everyone accountable.

## SECTION 7: Cost Optimization Checklist

Review this quarterly. Go through each area and check whether you're leaving money on the table.

### Labor (typically 50-60% of expenses)

- [ ] Are any employees consistently under-scheduled? (Paying for availability without utilization)
- [ ] Are any classrooms over-staffed relative to ratios? (Could consolidate during low-attendance hours)
- [ ] Is overtime planned or reactive? (Reactive OT costs 1.5x; plan ahead)
- [ ] Are shift start/end times aligned with child arrival/departure patterns?
- [ ] Could any admin tasks be batched to reduce total staff hours?

### CACFP Reimbursement (typically $40K-80K/year)

- [ ] Are all meal counts submitted on time? (Each missed count = $3-8 lost per child)
- [ ] Are income eligibility forms current for all families? (Determines reimbursement rate)
- [ ] Are infant feeding records complete? (Infant meals reimburse at a higher rate)
- [ ] Is the menu compliant with meal pattern requirements? (Non-compliant meals aren't reimbursed)

### Enrollment Revenue

- [ ] Are there empty spots in any classroom? (Each empty spot = $800-1,200/month lost)
- [ ] Are stalled leads being followed up? (Check the pipeline for leads older than 7 days)
- [ ] Are authorizations expiring without renewal? (Lapsed auth = child drops out)
- [ ] Is the waitlist being actively managed?

### Supplies and Operations

- [ ] Are supply orders consolidated? (Bulk ordering reduces per-unit cost)
- [ ] Are utilities reasonable for the season? (Compare month-over-month)
- [ ] Are any subscriptions or services unused? (Cancel what you don't use)
- [ ] Is food waste being tracked? (Over-ordering = money in the trash)

## SECTION 8: Strategic Planning

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Strategy

### Mission, Vision, and Values

The platform stores your center's mission, vision, and values so they're visible to all staff:

- **Mission** -- Why the center exists. What you do every day.
- **Vision** -- Where you're headed. What the center looks like in 3-5 years.
- **Values** -- How you operate. The non-negotiable principles.

Review these annually. If they feel stale or disconnected from daily reality, revise them.

### SWOT Analysis Tool

Run a SWOT analysis quarterly to stay aware of your position:

| Category | Prompt |
|---|---|
| **Strengths** | What do we do better than competitors? What do families love about us? |
| **Weaknesses** | Where do we consistently struggle? What complaints keep recurring? |
| **Opportunities** | What's changing in our market? Are there unserved needs nearby? |
| **Threats** | What could hurt enrollment? What's our biggest financial risk? |

**How to use it:**
1. Go to Strategy > SWOT
2. Enter items in each quadrant
3. The system saves your history so you can compare quarter over quarter
4. Look for patterns: a weakness that persists for 3 quarters needs structural change, not more effort

### Strategic Priorities

Set 3-5 priorities per quarter:
1. Define the priority (specific and measurable)
2. Set a target date
3. Assign an owner (yourself or a director)
4. Link to tasks that support the priority
5. Review progress monthly
