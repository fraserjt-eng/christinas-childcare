## SECTION 2: Staff Scheduling

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Scheduling

### Building a Weekly Schedule

1. Open the Scheduling page
2. Select the week you're building (aim for next week by Wednesday)
3. You'll see a grid: staff names on the left, days across the top
4. Drag and drop shifts to assign staff to time blocks
5. Assign each staff member to a classroom

### Ratio Compliance View

As you build the schedule, the system checks staff-to-child ratios in real time:

| Age Group | Required Ratio | Max Group Size |
|---|---|---|
| Infants (0-16 months) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddlers (16-33 months) | 1:7 | 14 |
| Preschool (33 months - 5 years) | 1:10 | 20 |

- **Green** -- Ratio met for all hours
- **Yellow** -- Ratio is tight (one absence breaks it)
- **Red** -- Ratio violation. Must fix before publishing.

### Labor Cost Projection

The schedule page shows projected labor costs for the week as you build it. Watch for:
- Overtime flags (any staff approaching 40 hours)
- Coverage gaps (time blocks with no assigned staff)
- Cost vs. budget comparison

### Publishing

1. Verify all shifts are filled and ratios are green
2. Tap "Publish Schedule"
3. Staff receive a notification with their upcoming shifts

**Habit:** Draft next week's schedule by Wednesday. Publish by Thursday at noon. Check coverage requests daily.

## SECTION 3: CACFP Compliance

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Compliance > CACFP Dashboard

### What the Dashboard Shows

- **Audit Readiness Score** -- Percentage score (target: 90%+). This measures how prepared you are if an auditor walked in today.
- **Meal Count Completion** -- Which classrooms submitted on time, which are late or missing
- **Documentation Status** -- Required forms, menus, income eligibility forms
- **Reimbursement Tracking** -- Estimated monthly reimbursement based on submitted counts

### CACFP Compliance Checklist

Review this weekly (every Monday):

- [ ] All meal counts submitted for previous week (no gaps)
- [ ] Current month's menu posted and matches served meals
- [ ] Income eligibility forms current for all enrolled families
- [ ] Meal pattern requirements met (grains, protein, fruit, vegetable, milk)
- [ ] Special dietary accommodations documented
- [ ] Attendance records match meal count records (no meals counted for absent children)
- [ ] Infant meal records include formula/breast milk documentation

### What Each Color Means

- **Green (90-100%)** -- Audit-ready. Maintain this.
- **Yellow (70-89%)** -- Gaps exist. Address this week.
- **Red (below 70%)** -- Significant risk. Fix today.

**Cost context:** CACFP reimbursement can be $40,000-80,000 per year for a center this size. A failed audit suspends that income.

## SECTION 4: Newsletter Creation

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Communication > Newsletters

### Creating a Newsletter

1. Tap "New Newsletter"
2. Choose a template:
   - Weekly Update (standard)
   - Monthly Spotlight
   - Special Announcement
3. Fill in sections:
   - **Header/greeting** -- Keep it warm and brief
   - **This Week's Highlights** -- Activities, milestones, photos
   - **Upcoming Events** -- Field trips, closures, spirit days
   - **Menu Preview** -- Next week's meals
   - **Reminders** -- Supplies needed, policy reminders
4. Preview the newsletter
5. Schedule send time or send immediately
6. Track read receipts after sending

**Habit:** Send the weekly newsletter every Friday by 3:00 PM. Include at least one photo. Parents who feel informed stay enrolled.

## SECTION 5: Enrollment Pipeline

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Enrollment > Pipeline

### Funnel Stages

Every inquiry moves through these stages:

1. **New Inquiry** -- Someone called, emailed, or submitted the website form
2. **Tour Scheduled** -- Date and time confirmed
3. **Tour Completed** -- Family visited the center
4. **Paperwork Sent** -- Enrollment forms sent to family
5. **Paperwork Received** -- Forms returned and reviewed
6. **Enrolled** -- Child has a start date and classroom assignment

### Managing the Pipeline

- New inquiries appear automatically from the website form
- Move families between stages by updating their status
- Set follow-up reminders for stalled leads
- The system flags any family that's been in one stage for more than 7 days

### Follow-Up Timing

| Stage | Follow-Up Window |
|---|---|
| New Inquiry | Respond within 24 hours |
| Tour Scheduled | Confirm 24 hours before |
| Tour Completed | Thank-you message within 2 hours, follow-up at 48 hours |
| Paperwork Sent | Check in at 5 days if not returned |

**Cost context:** Each enrolled child represents $800-1,200 per month in revenue. Converting just 2 additional leads per quarter adds $20,000-30,000 per year.

## SECTION 6: HR Quick Reference

**Go to:** Admin Portal > HR > Documents

### Document Templates Available

- Offer letter
- Written warning
- Performance improvement plan
- Termination notice
- Policy acknowledgment
- Emergency contact update form

### Using Templates

1. Select the template type
2. Fill in the employee name, date, and details
3. Preview the document
4. Save to the employee's file
5. Print if a physical signature is needed

### Discipline Records

All discipline actions should be documented in the system:
1. Go to the employee's profile
2. Select "Add HR Action"
3. Choose the type (verbal warning, written warning, PIP, etc.)
4. Enter the date, description, and any follow-up required
5. Save. The record is timestamped and permanent.

### Onboarding Pathways

When a new hire starts:
1. Go to HR > Onboarding
2. Create a new onboarding pathway for the employee
3. The system generates a checklist: background check, orientation, training modules, policy signatures, certification verification
4. Track completion at day 1, week 1, and month 1

**Habit:** File all HR actions the same day they happen. Memory fades; documentation doesn't.

## SECTION 7: Staff Development Tracking

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Staff > Development

### What You Can See

- Each employee's current certifications and expiry dates
- Training hours completed this year vs. required
- Upcoming expiration alerts (90/60/30 days)
- Training history and completed modules

### Certification Expiry Management

The system sends alerts at three thresholds:
- **90 days out** -- Yellow. Start planning.
- **60 days out** -- Orange. Schedule the renewal.
- **30 days out** -- Red. Urgent action needed.

**Your responsibility:** Review the staff development dashboard every Monday. If any staff member has an orange or red certification, address it that week. An expired certification means that person cannot legally work in ratio.

## SECTION 8: Authorization Tracking

**Go to:** Admin Portal > Compliance > Authorizations

### What This Tracks

State childcare authorizations (county/state funding) for enrolled children. Each authorization has:
- Start and end dates
- Authorized hours per week
- Funding source
- Renewal status

### Alert Timeline

| Alert | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Green | Current, 60+ days remaining | No action |
| Yellow | 30-60 days until expiry | Remind family to start renewal |
| Red | Under 30 days or expired | Contact family immediately; revenue at risk |

### Revenue-at-Risk View

The dashboard calculates how much monthly revenue depends on authorizations that are expiring soon. Use this to prioritize which families need follow-up first.
