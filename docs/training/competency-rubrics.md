# Competency Rubrics

Christina's Child Care Center Training Curriculum

Observable skills checklists for employees, directors, and owner. Each competency is rated at three levels based on observable behaviors, not self-reported confidence.

---

## Rating Levels

| Level | Definition | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Guided** | Needs help or reminders to complete | Requires prompting, makes frequent errors, asks for help regularly |
| **Independent** | Completes consistently without support | Performs correctly and on time without reminders or supervision |
| **Mentor** | Can teach others and troubleshoot problems | Trains new staff, catches system errors, explains the "why" behind the task |

---

## Employee Rubric (8 Competencies)

All staff in Tier 2 (Employee/Staff) are assessed on these 8 competencies.

---

### Competency 1: Clock In/Out Accurately Every Shift

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Forgets to clock in or out at least once per week. Needs reminders from the director. Time entries require manual correction regularly. |
| **Independent** | Clocks in within 5 minutes of shift start and out at shift end every day. No manual corrections needed. Notifies director immediately if a clock error occurs. |
| **Mentor** | Shows new staff how to use the kiosk for time entry. Notices when colleagues forget to clock in and reminds them. Explains the payroll and overtime implications of missed punches. |

---

### Competency 2: Submit All Meal Counts Before Deadline

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Needs reminders about deadlines. Misses 1-2 meal counts per week. Needs help using the pre-fill feature. Submits counts with errors (wrong child count, wrong meal type). |
| **Independent** | Submits all 4 meal types on time daily. Uses pre-fill correctly and adjusts for absent children. Corrects own errors before submitting. Never lets a deadline indicator turn red. |
| **Mentor** | Trains new staff on the meal count process and deadlines. Catches pre-fill errors before submission. Explains CACFP reimbursement impact to others. Covers meal count duties when colleagues are absent. |

---

### Competency 3: Upload 3+ Tagged Photos Per Shift

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Uploads fewer than 3 photos most shifts. Forgets to add activity tags. Captions are blank or generic ("kids playing"). Needs reminders to take photos during activities. |
| **Independent** | Uploads 3-5 photos per shift with accurate activity tags and descriptive captions. Takes photos during different activity blocks. Follows the approval workflow before photos go to parents. |
| **Mentor** | Takes high-quality photos that capture engagement and learning. Coaches new staff on what makes a good classroom photo. Suggests new activity tags to the director. Batch uploads efficiently during transitions. |

---

### Competency 4: Complete All Assigned Tasks by End of Shift

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Leaves tasks incomplete without notifying the director. Does not check the task board at shift start. Misses priority-based ordering; does easy tasks first regardless of urgency. |
| **Independent** | Checks task board at shift start. Completes all assigned tasks by end of shift. Works through urgent items first. Marks tasks complete in the system immediately after finishing. Uses nap-time window for admin tasks. |
| **Mentor** | Identifies tasks that need to be created and suggests them to the director. Helps colleagues prioritize their task boards. Develops efficient task routines that others adopt. Flags systemic task issues (e.g., "this daily task takes longer than estimated"). |

---

### Competency 5: File Incident Report Within 30 Minutes of Occurrence

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Delays incident reports past 30 minutes. Leaves fields incomplete (missing time, location, or description). Assigns incorrect severity level. Forgets to notify parents through the system. |
| **Independent** | Files complete incident reports within 30 minutes. Selects correct severity level. Includes all required fields: child name, time, location, description, injuries, first aid, staff present. Notifies parent through the system same day. |
| **Mentor** | Helps new staff file their first incident reports correctly. Reviews reports for completeness before submission. Explains which incidents are licensure-reportable. Identifies patterns in incident data and raises them with the director. |

---

### Competency 6: Maintain Current Certifications in System

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Does not know expiration dates for own certifications. Ignores system alerts. Discovers expired certifications only when the director flags them. Does not log training hours. |
| **Independent** | Knows expiration dates for all required certifications (CPR, First Aid, food handler, mandatory reporter). Responds to 90/60/30-day alerts by scheduling renewal. Logs training hours in the system promptly. |
| **Mentor** | Helps new staff understand certification requirements and timelines. Shares information about available training classes. Reminds colleagues when their renewal alerts appear. Maintains a training resource list for the team. |

---

### Competency 7: Respond to Parent Messages Within 4 Hours

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Does not check messages during the shift. Leaves parent messages unanswered for 24+ hours. Responses are incomplete or unclear. Forgets to flag messages that need director attention. |
| **Independent** | Checks messages at shift start and end. Responds to parent messages within 4 business hours. Responses are professional, clear, and address the parent's question. Escalates messages that require director input. |
| **Mentor** | Models professional communication tone for new staff. Helps draft responses to sensitive parent messages. Suggests message templates for common questions. De-escalates concerned parent messages before they become complaints. |

---

### Competency 8: Check Dashboard Alerts at Start of Shift

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Does not open the dashboard at shift start. Misses alerts about attendance changes, task assignments, or ratio warnings. Relies entirely on verbal updates from colleagues. |
| **Independent** | Opens the dashboard within 10 minutes of shift start. Reviews alerts, attendance status, and task assignments. Acts on any red or yellow alerts immediately. Verbally confirms key information with the outgoing shift. |
| **Mentor** | Explains dashboard layout and alert meanings to new staff. Has developed a personal shift-start routine that others follow. Identifies when dashboard data looks wrong and reports it. Uses dashboard data to plan the day proactively. |

## Director Rubric (14 Competencies)

Directors (Tier 3) are assessed on all 8 employee competencies above, plus the following 6 director-level competencies.

---

### Competency 9: Build Compliant Weekly Schedule Under Budget

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Creates schedules with ratio gaps that require last-minute fixes. Does not check labor cost projection before publishing. Schedules result in unplanned overtime. Publishes schedules late (after Thursday noon). |
| **Independent** | Drafts schedule by Wednesday. Verifies ratio compliance for every hour across all classrooms. Checks labor cost projection and adjusts to stay within budget. Publishes by Thursday noon. Processes coverage requests within 24 hours. |
| **Mentor** | Teaches new directors the scheduling workflow, including staggered staffing and ratio-based coverage. Identifies optimization opportunities (e.g., "we have 2 extra staff hours on Wednesday that could shift to Thursday's gap"). Creates scheduling templates for common patterns. |

---

### Competency 10: Process Enrollment Inquiry Within 48 Hours

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Inquiries sit in the pipeline without follow-up for 3+ days. Does not capture complete information during initial contact. Does not schedule tours proactively. Pipeline has multiple stalled leads with no notes. |
| **Independent** | Responds to every inquiry within 48 hours. Captures complete intake data (parent name, child age, desired start, schedule preference, referral source). Schedules tours during the initial conversation when possible. Updates pipeline status and adds follow-up notes. |
| **Mentor** | Converts inquiries to tours at a rate above 50%. Coaches staff on phone inquiry handling. Develops follow-up message templates. Tracks conversion rates and suggests pipeline improvements. Recovers stalled leads through personalized outreach. |

---

### Competency 11: Achieve 90%+ CACFP Audit Readiness Score

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Does not check the compliance dashboard regularly. Audit readiness score fluctuates below 85%. Cannot locate required documents during a practice audit. Needs help interpreting compliance dashboard items. |
| **Independent** | Checks compliance dashboard every Monday. Maintains audit readiness score at 90% or above. Addresses yellow items same day and red items within 1 hour. Can pull all required audit documents within 5 minutes. Generates compliance reports for the owner. |
| **Mentor** | Maintains consistent 95%+ audit readiness. Conducts mock audits with staff quarterly. Trains new directors on CACFP requirements and the compliance dashboard. Identifies compliance trends and implements preventive measures. |

---

### Competency 12: Conduct Monthly 1:1 with Each Staff Member Using HR Tools

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Skips monthly 1:1 meetings or holds them inconsistently. Does not document conversations in the HR module. Performance feedback is vague ("you're doing great" or "you need to improve" without specifics). |
| **Independent** | Holds a documented 1:1 with each staff member every month. Uses the HR module to record conversation topics, goals, and follow-up items. Provides specific, behavior-based feedback. References previous 1:1 notes to track progress on prior goals. |
| **Mentor** | Uses HR data (task completion, incident reports, certification status) to prepare for 1:1 conversations. Connects individual development to center goals. Coaches other directors on effective 1:1 practices. Creates development pathways for high-performing staff. |

---

### Competency 13: Send Weekly Newsletter by Friday

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Newsletters go out inconsistently (every 2-3 weeks) or not at all. Content is minimal (text-only, no photos or sections). Does not use templates. Does not check read receipts. |
| **Independent** | Sends a newsletter every Friday by 3:00 PM. Includes 3+ sections: photos, upcoming events, menu, classroom highlights. Uses templates. Reviews read receipts the following Monday to see engagement. |
| **Mentor** | Creates newsletter templates that other directors use. Maintains a monthly content calendar. Writes newsletters that parents forward to prospective families. Includes parent spotlight features and community-building content. Tracks which content types get highest read rates. |

---

### Competency 14: Resolve Ratio Violations Within 15 Minutes

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Does not notice ratio violations until told. Takes 30+ minutes to resolve coverage issues. Relies solely on the owner to find backup staff. Does not have a coverage plan for common scenarios (callouts, late arrivals). |
| **Independent** | Monitors ratio dashboard at every transition (arrival, nap, departure). When a violation occurs, contacts backup staff or reassigns coverage within 15 minutes. Maintains a current substitute/backup list. Documents the violation and resolution. |
| **Mentor** | Prevents most ratio violations through proactive scheduling and monitoring. Has created a coverage protocol that other staff follow when the director is unavailable. Trains staff to self-monitor ratios and escalate before violations occur. Analyzes ratio data to identify recurring vulnerability windows. |

## Owner Rubric (20 Competencies)

The owner (Tier 4) is assessed on all 14 director competencies above, plus the following 6 owner-level competencies.

---

### Competency 15: Review Budget vs. Actual Monthly (Variance Under 10%)

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Reviews budget quarterly or less. Cannot identify which categories are over budget without the accountant's help. Does not use the budget dashboard; waits for external reports. Variances exceed 10% in multiple categories before detection. |
| **Independent** | Reviews budget versus actual on the 1st of every month. Investigates any category variance exceeding 10%. Takes corrective action within the same month (vendor renegotiation, purchase holds, schedule adjustment). Exports data for the accountant as needed. |
| **Mentor** | Maintains all budget categories within 5% variance. Uses historical budget data to set increasingly accurate annual targets. Teaches directors to monitor their site-level spending. Connects budget performance to strategic decisions (e.g., "we saved $8,000 on labor through scheduling optimization, which funds the playground upgrade"). |

---

### Competency 16: Run Revenue Forecast and Scenario Model Quarterly

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Does not use the forecasting tool. Makes financial decisions based on current-month data only. Cannot describe the center's breakeven enrollment number. Has never run a "what-if" scenario. |
| **Independent** | Runs a revenue forecast at least quarterly. Knows the breakeven enrollment number for each site. Models scenarios before making pricing, staffing, or expansion decisions (e.g., "what happens if we lose 3 families" or "what if we raise rates by $50"). Uses forecast data in strategic planning. |
| **Mentor** | Runs monthly forecasts and proactively shares projections with stakeholders. Models multiple scenarios before any major decision and documents the analysis. Uses forecast accuracy (projected versus actual) to improve future projections. Teaches directors to interpret revenue reports and understand their site's financial contribution. |

---

### Competency 17: Update Strategic Plan Quarterly

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Has a strategic plan document that has not been updated in 6+ months. Cannot name current strategic priorities without looking them up. Does not connect daily decisions to strategic goals. SWOT analysis is outdated or nonexistent. |
| **Independent** | Reviews and updates the strategic plan every quarter. Conducts a SWOT refresh at each quarterly review. Tracks progress on 2-3 measurable priorities with specific deadlines. Translates strategic priorities into monthly action items that appear on the task board. |
| **Mentor** | Strategic plan is a living document that visibly drives decisions. Involves directors in quarterly strategic reviews. Adjusts priorities based on data rather than reacting to the latest crisis. Can articulate a 12-month strategic narrative: where the center was, where it is, and where it is heading. |

---

### Competency 18: Maintain Zero Expired Staff Certifications

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Discovers expired certifications reactively (during a licensing visit or when a staff member mentions it). Does not review the certification dashboard regularly. No system for staggering renewal dates across staff. |
| **Independent** | Reviews the certification dashboard monthly. Responds to 90-day alerts by confirming renewal plans with staff. Ensures no certifications expire without a scheduled renewal. Coordinates training schedules to avoid multiple staff attending classes simultaneously. |
| **Mentor** | Has implemented a staggered renewal calendar so certifications never cluster. Maintains relationships with training providers for priority enrollment. Has built a "certification health" metric into the center's monthly review. Coaches directors to own certification tracking for their teams. |

---

### Competency 19: Cross-Site Dashboard Review Daily

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Relies on phone calls or site visits to know what is happening at each location. Does not open the cross-site dashboard regularly. Learns about issues at the other site late (after they have escalated). |
| **Independent** | Reviews the cross-site dashboard every morning before making site visit decisions. Manages by exception: only digs into sites showing yellow or red indicators. Uses cross-site comparison to identify best practices at one site that could transfer to the other. Reduces unnecessary travel between sites. |
| **Mentor** | Has reduced weekly cross-site travel from 15+ hours to under 5 by using the dashboard for routine oversight. Uses cross-site data in director 1:1 meetings to drive performance conversations. Identifies trends visible only through cross-site comparison (e.g., "Brooklyn Park's PM snack count drops every Thursday; Crystal's is consistent"). Delegates routine monitoring to directors while retaining exception-based oversight. |

---

### Competency 20: Delegate 60%+ of Operational Tasks

| Level | Observable Indicators |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Guided** | Performs most operational tasks personally. Spends less than 2 hours per week on strategic work. Task board shows the owner assigned to routine items that directors or staff could handle. Frequently says "it's faster if I just do it myself." |
| **Independent** | Delegates routine operational tasks to directors and staff with clear expectations and deadlines. Spends at least 5 hours per week on strategic work (planning, financial review, enrollment strategy, community partnerships). Task board shows the owner assigned primarily to strategic and decision-level items. |
| **Mentor** | Has built systems that run without daily owner involvement for routine operations (meal counts, scheduling, task management, parent communication). Directors independently resolve 90%+ of operational issues. Owner focuses on growth, financial strategy, and organizational development. Can be absent for a week with no operational disruption. |

### What to Do at Each Level

| Level | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Guided** | Schedule a focused practice session on this competency. Pair with a Mentor-level colleague. Reassess in 2 weeks. If still Guided after 30 days, create a Growth Plan. |
| **Independent** | Maintain current performance. Include in quarterly review. No additional action needed unless regression occurs. |
| **Mentor** | Assign as trainer for this competency during new hire onboarding. Include in facilitator rotation for PD sessions. Recognize in staff meetings. |

### Assessment Conversation Guide

When conducting a competency assessment, follow this structure:

1. **Review the indicators together.** Read the Guided, Independent, and Mentor descriptions for each competency with the staff member present.
2. **Ask for self-assessment first.** "Where do you think you are on this one?" Listen for specific examples.
3. **Share your observation.** Reference specific, recent behaviors. "Last week I noticed you submitted meal counts by 9:25 AM three days in a row. That is consistent Independent-level performance."
4. **Agree on the rating.** If you disagree, use the observable indicators as the tiebreaker, not feelings.
5. **Identify one competency to develop.** Do not try to improve everything at once. Pick the one with the highest operational impact and create a Growth Plan for it.
6. **Set the next assessment date.** Write it down. Both parties commit.
