# Unit 1: Getting Started

**Modules:** M01 Welcome & Login, M02 Navigating Your Portal, M03 Your Profile & Settings, M04 Kiosk Check-In/Check-Out
**Audience:** All roles (Parents, Employees, Directors, Owner)
**Total Time:** 45-60 minutes (self-paced + facilitated kiosk demo)

Foundation skills. Everyone starts here, regardless of role. The goal is simple: get every person logged in, oriented, and confident enough to stop asking "where do I find that?" within their first week.

---

## M01: Welcome & Login

**Format:** Self-paced (5 min) + paper quick-start card
**Roles:** All (Tiers 1-4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Print one quick-start card per participant (see Handout Content below)
- Confirm that every participant has a user account created in the system
- Have 2-3 devices available for anyone who forgets their phone
- Test the login flow yourself that morning to catch any issues

**What to demo:**
- Open the platform on your phone and on a laptop side by side
- Show the difference between the three portal entry points: /dashboard (parent), /employee (staff), /admin (director/owner)
- Show what happens when someone tries to access a portal they don't have permission for (the /access-denied page)

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**For Parents:**
1. Open your phone browser and go to christinas-childcare.vercel.app
2. Tap "Parent Login" on the homepage
3. Enter your email address and the temporary password from your welcome packet
4. You will land on /dashboard, which is your home base
5. Bookmark this page on your phone (share icon, then "Add to Home Screen")

**For Employees:**
1. Open your phone browser and go to christinas-childcare.vercel.app
2. Tap "Staff Login" on the homepage
3. Enter your email and temporary password
4. You will land on /employee, which is your daily command center
5. Add to home screen so it works like an app

**For Directors and Owner:**
1. Same login process, but your account has access to /admin as well
2. After logging in, you can switch between /employee (your daily view) and /admin (your management view)
3. Bookmark both

**Password Reset:**
1. On the login screen, tap "Forgot Password"
2. Enter your email
3. Check your inbox (and spam folder) for the reset link
4. Create a new password (at least 8 characters, one number)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Using the wrong portal URL | You see pages you don't recognize, or get an access denied screen | Always start from your bookmarked link |
| Sharing login credentials | Someone else makes changes under your name | Every person gets their own account, no exceptions |
| Forgetting to bookmark | You type the URL wrong every time | Add to home screen on day one |
| Using an old browser | Layout looks broken, buttons don't work | Update Chrome or Safari before training |

## M02: Navigating Your Portal

**Format:** Self-paced (10 min) with interactive walkthrough
**Roles:** All (Tiers 1-4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Prepare a "scavenger hunt" list (see Assessment below) printed on half-sheets
- Have the platform open on a projector or large screen for group walkthrough
- Know the mobile bottom navigation layout for each portal

**What to demo:**
- Show the bottom navigation bar on mobile for each portal
- Demonstrate the "two-click rule": any important page should be reachable in two taps or fewer
- Show the dashboard alerts section and explain what each color means
- Walk through the difference between sidebar navigation (desktop) and bottom tab navigation (mobile)

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**Parent Portal (/dashboard):**

The parent dashboard is your home screen. From here you can reach everything.

1. **Dashboard home** (/dashboard): Shows your child's daily summary, recent photos, and any alerts
2. **Children** (/dashboard/children): Tap the "Children" tab to see profiles, allergies, and emergency contacts
3. **Photos** (/dashboard/photos): Tap "Photos" to see pictures from your child's day
4. **Messages** (/dashboard/messages): Tap "Messages" to communicate with teachers and directors
5. **Calendar** (/dashboard/calendar): Tap "Calendar" to see events, closures, and reminders
6. **News** (/dashboard/news): Center announcements and newsletters
7. **Notifications** (/dashboard/notifications): All your alerts in one place
8. **Documents** (/dashboard/documents): Forms, policies, and signed documents
9. **Progress** (/dashboard/progress): Your child's developmental milestones

1. **Employee home** (/employee): Today's schedule, assigned tasks, and alerts

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |

| Not switching to admin when needed | Directors stay in employee view and miss management alerts | Know when to switch portals based on the task |
| Scrolling past the alert banner | Critical info sits at the top of the dashboard | Read the colored banners before doing anything else |

## M03: Your Profile & Settings

**Format:** Self-paced (10 min)
**Roles:** All (Tiers 1-4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Have a sample "complete" profile open to show what 100% looks like
- Prepare a checklist of required fields by role
- Know where notification preferences live for each portal

**What to demo:**
- Show a profile at 60% complete vs. 100% complete
- Demonstrate the emergency contact section and why it matters for licensing
- Show notification preference toggles and explain which ones are critical (incident alerts, schedule changes)
- Show the photo upload process

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**For Parents (/dashboard/children):**

1. Go to /dashboard
2. Tap "Children" in the bottom navigation
3. Tap your child's name to open their profile
4. Review and update each section:
   - **Basic Info:** Full legal name, date of birth, gender
   - **Allergies:** List every allergy, including severity (this is critical; teachers see this in real-time)
   - **Medical:** Medications, doctor's name and number, insurance info
   - **Emergency Contacts:** At least 2 contacts besides yourself, with phone numbers. These people are authorized for pickup.
   - **Authorized Pickup:** List every person allowed to pick up your child. Anyone not on this list will be turned away.
   - **Dietary:** Food restrictions, preferences, and cultural considerations
5. Tap "Save" after each section
6. Go to /dashboard/notifications to configure your notification preferences

**For Employees (/employee/profile):**

1. Go to /employee
2. Tap "Profile" in the navigation
3. Review and update each section:
   - **Personal Info:** Name, phone, email, address
   - **Emergency Contacts:** At least 1 emergency contact with phone number
   - **Certifications:** Upload current CPR/First Aid, background check, and any required training certificates
   - **Photo:** Upload a headshot (parents see this when viewing classroom staff)
   - **Notification Preferences:** Configure which alerts you receive
4. Tap "Save" after each section

**For Directors/Owner (/admin/settings):**

1. Go to /admin/settings
2. Review center-level settings:
   - **General** (/admin/settings): Center name, address, phone, hours of operation
   - **Users** (/admin/settings/users): Manage user accounts and role assignments
   - **Roles** (/admin/settings/roles): Define what each role can access
   - **Security** (/admin/settings/security): Password policies, session timeouts
   - **Audit** (/admin/settings/audit): View who changed what and when
   - **Backup** (/admin/settings/backup): Data export and backup schedule

1. Why does the platform need emergency contacts from both parents and staff?

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Leaving allergy fields blank | Teacher doesn't know about a peanut allergy; this is a safety emergency | Fill allergies on day one, before the child's first meal |
| Only listing one emergency contact | If that person doesn't answer, nobody can be reached | Minimum 2 contacts; 3 is better |
| Skipping the photo upload | Parents don't know who is caring for their child | Upload your headshot during onboarding |
| Not updating after a move or phone change | Emergency contact info is wrong when it matters most | Update the platform the same day anything changes |
| Ignoring notification settings | You either get too many alerts or miss critical ones | Spend 2 minutes configuring preferences during setup |

## M04: Kiosk Check-In/Check-Out

**Format:** Facilitated demo (5 min) + paper card with PIN
**Roles:** Parents (Tier 1), Employees (Tier 2), Directors (Tier 3)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Set up the kiosk device at the front entrance (tablet or dedicated screen)
- Generate PINs for all participants and print them on individual cards
- Have a backup paper sign-in sheet ready in case the kiosk goes down (this is also a good teaching moment about why digital is better)
- Test the auto-reset timer so you know how long it takes to return to the PIN screen

**What to demo:**
- Walk through one complete check-in at the kiosk, narrating each step
- Show what happens when you enter a wrong PIN (error message, retry)
- Show the auto-reset timer (the screen returns to the PIN entry after inactivity)
- Demonstrate checking in multiple children from the same family
- Show the "checked in" confirmation screen and what info it displays
- Show what the attendance dashboard (/admin/attendance) looks like in real-time as someone checks in

**Important context for families:**
This kiosk replaces the paper sign-in binder. The old way had problems: illegible handwriting, missing entries, and no way to verify times. The digital kiosk timestamps every check-in and check-out to the minute. This matters because accurate attendance drives CACFP meal reimbursement, and each child's daily attendance is worth $2-5 in federal meal funding.

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**Parent Check-In (morning drop-off):**

1. Walk up to the kiosk at the front entrance (/kiosk)
2. The screen shows a PIN entry keypad
3. Enter your 4-digit family PIN (printed on your quick-start card)
4. The screen shows your children's names with a "Check In" button next to each one
5. Tap "Check In" next to each child you are dropping off
6. The screen confirms: "[Child Name] checked in at [time]"
7. If you have multiple children, check in each one before walking away
8. The screen resets automatically after a few seconds of inactivity

**Parent Check-Out (afternoon pickup):**

1. Same process: enter your PIN at the kiosk
2. This time, your children show a "Check Out" button
3. Tap "Check Out" next to each child you are picking up
4. The screen confirms the checkout time
5. If someone else is picking up (authorized on your child's profile), they use their own PIN

**What the director sees:**

When a parent checks in at the kiosk, the /admin/attendance page updates instantly. The director can see:
- Which children are present right now
- What time each child arrived
- Which children are expected but haven't arrived
- Total head count by classroom
- Current staff-to-child ratio implications

### Discussion Questions

1. What should you do if the kiosk is down when you arrive? (Answer: notify staff immediately; use the paper backup, then the director will enter the time manually)
2. Why is the exact check-in time important? (Answer: CACFP reimbursement requires attendance records that match meal counts)
3. What happens if your sister picks up your child but isn't on the authorized pickup list? (Answer: staff cannot release the child; this is a safety and licensing requirement)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Forgetting your PIN | You can't check in; creates a line at the door | Keep your quick-start card in your wallet or phone case |
| Walking past the kiosk without checking in | Child is marked absent; meal counts won't match | Make check-in part of your drop-off routine: kiosk first, then classroom |
| Checking in but forgetting to check out | Records show child is still at the center overnight | Staff should remind at pickup; directors should review unclosed check-ins at 6 PM |
| Letting another parent use your PIN | Wrong family gets credited; attendance data is corrupted | Your PIN is yours alone; unauthorized pickup is a safety issue |
| Not checking in all children | One child's attendance is missing | Always verify each child's name shows "checked in" before walking away |

### Handout Content: Kiosk Quick Card

**Kiosk Check-In / Check-Out**

Your Family PIN: ________

**Drop-off:**
1. Enter PIN on the kiosk screen
2. Tap "Check In" for each child
3. Wait for the confirmation message

2. Tap "Check Out" for each child

**If the kiosk is down:** Tell the front desk staff immediately. They will record your time manually.

### Assessment: Kiosk Competency Check

Each participant demonstrates at the kiosk with a supervisor watching:

- [ ] Enters PIN correctly on first or second try
- [ ] Checks in the correct child(ren)
- [ ] Reads and confirms the check-in message
- [ ] Can explain what to do if the kiosk is offline
- [ ] Understands that only authorized people can pick up

## Unit 1 Summary

After completing this unit, every participant should be able to:

1. Log into the correct portal without assistance
2. Navigate to any page in their portal within two taps
3. Have a 100% complete profile (or child profile, for parents)
4. Check in and check out at the kiosk confidently
5. Know who to ask when something doesn't work

**Next up:** Unit 2, Daily Rhythms. That is where the real work of running the center lives: attendance tracking, meal counts, photos, tasks, and daily reports.
