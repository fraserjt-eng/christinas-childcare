# Unit 3: Communication & Family Engagement

**Modules:** M10 Parent-Staff Messaging, M11 Newsletters & Announcements, M12 Parent Portal Mastery, M13 Notification Management
**Audience:** All roles (varies by module)
**Total Time:** 1.5-2 hours (self-paced + facilitated best practices session)

Communication is the difference between a center where families feel informed and a center where families feel anxious. Every unanswered message, every missing newsletter, every blank field in a child's profile creates a small crack in trust. This unit closes those cracks.

---

## M10: Parent-Staff Messaging

**Format:** Self-paced (10 min)
**Roles:** Parents (Tier 1), Employees (Tier 2), Directors (Tier 3), Owner (Tier 4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Create a test conversation between a parent account and a staff account so participants can see what a thread looks like
- Prepare a list of "appropriate" vs. "not appropriate" message examples (see discussion questions)
- Know the response time expectation: 4 hours during business hours

**What to demo:**
- Send a message from the parent side and show it arriving on the staff side
- Show how conversation history is preserved (no more lost cubby notes)
- Demonstrate how directors can view all conversations in their center for support and oversight
- Show the difference between a direct message and a center-wide announcement

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**Parent view (/dashboard/messages):**

1. Go to /dashboard
2. Tap "Messages" in the navigation
3. You see your conversation threads listed by teacher/staff member name
4. To send a new message:
   - Tap "New Message"
   - Select the recipient (your child's teacher, the director, or the office)
   - Type your message
   - Tap "Send"
5. Messages are timestamped. You can scroll back through the full conversation history.
6. You will receive a notification when someone replies (if notifications are enabled; see M13)

**Employee view (from the employee portal):**

1. Go to /employee
2. Open the Messages section from your dashboard or navigation
3. You see conversations from parents in your classroom
4. To reply:
   - Tap the conversation
   - Read the full thread
   - Type your response
   - Tap "Send"
5. Response expectation: respond within 4 hours during business hours (6:30 AM - 6:00 PM)
6. If a message requires director involvement (complaints, medical questions, schedule conflicts), forward it or flag it

**Director/Owner view (/admin/messaging):**

1. Go to /admin/messaging
2. This view shows all conversations across the center
3. You can filter by:
   - Classroom
   - Staff member
   - Date range
   - Unanswered messages (this is your most important filter)
4. The "unanswered" count tells you how many parent messages have been waiting more than 4 hours
5. You can jump into any conversation to reply directly or to coach a staff member on their response

### Discussion Questions

1. A parent messages at 9 AM asking if their child's rash has gotten worse. How quickly should this be answered? What if the classroom teacher is busy with the children? (Answer: health-related messages should be answered within 1 hour; the director should respond if the teacher cannot)
2. What kinds of conversations belong in the messaging system vs. in person? (Answer: routine updates, schedule questions, and quick check-ins belong in messaging. Serious behavior concerns, injury details, and sensitive family matters should happen face to face or by phone, then be documented)
3. Why is written communication history important for the center? (Answer: creates a record, prevents "he said/she said" disputes, protects both families and staff)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Not checking messages at start and end of shift | Parents feel ignored; issues escalate | Build message check into your arrival and departure routine |
| Responding to emotional messages immediately | Your response is reactive, not professional | Read the message. Wait 10 minutes. Then respond. |
| Using informal language or abbreviations | Professionalism drops; miscommunication increases | Write in complete sentences. Proofread before sending. |
| Leaving a message unanswered for a full day | Parent trust erodes; director has to intervene | 4-hour response window, maximum |
| Having sensitive conversations in writing | Creates a permanent record of something better discussed privately | Use messaging for routine topics; use phone or in-person for sensitive ones |

### Handout Content: Messaging Quick Reference

**Response time:** Within 4 hours during business hours.
**Tone:** Warm, professional, specific. Use the child's name. Answer the actual question.
**When to escalate:** Medical concerns, complaints about staff, legal or licensing questions. Forward to the director immediately.
**What not to send:** Nothing you wouldn't want read aloud in a licensing review.

## M11: Newsletters & Announcements

**Format:** Self-paced (20 min) + facilitated best practices session (30 min)
**Roles:** Directors (Tier 3), Owner (Tier 4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Have 2-3 example newsletters open (one good, one mediocre) to compare
- Prepare the weekly newsletter template structure (see below)
- Know how to access read receipts so you can show who opened the newsletter
- Prepare the "Friday by 3 PM" rhythm and explain why consistency matters more than perfection

**What to demo:**
- Create a newsletter from scratch using the template system
- Add sections: text, photos, upcoming events, menu highlights
- Show the scheduling feature (write on Thursday, schedule for Friday at 3 PM)
- Show read receipts after a newsletter is sent
- Show the announcement feature for urgent, time-sensitive notices (closures, weather)

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**Creating a newsletter (/admin/communications):**

1. Go to /admin/communications
2. Tap "New Newsletter"
3. Select a template or start from blank
4. Add sections (each is a building block):
   - **Header photo:** A standout image from the week
   - **Welcome/intro text:** 2-3 sentences about the week
   - **Highlights:** What happened this week (field trips, art projects, milestones)
   - **Upcoming events:** Next week's calendar items
   - **Menu preview:** What's on the lunch menu next week
   - **Parent spotlight:** (monthly) Highlight a family or volunteer
   - **Reminders:** Picture day, tuition due dates, early closures
5. Preview the newsletter to see how it looks on a phone
6. Choose to send now or schedule for later
7. Tap "Send" or "Schedule"

**Tracking engagement:**

1. After sending, go back to /admin/communications
2. Open the sent newsletter
3. View read receipts: how many families opened it, who hasn't opened it yet
4. For families who consistently don't open newsletters, consider:
   - Are their notification settings blocking email/push?
   - Do they need a different communication channel (text, paper)?

**Creating an announcement (urgent notices):**

1. Go to /admin/communications
2. Tap "New Announcement"
3. Write the message (keep it short and clear)
4. Select the audience: all families, specific classroom, or staff
5. Choose delivery: push notification + email (for urgent items like closures)
6. Tap "Send Now"

Announcements bypass the newsletter template. They are for time-sensitive information only: weather closures, health alerts, emergency schedule changes.

### Discussion Questions

1. What should always be in a weekly newsletter? What should never be in one?
2. A newsletter has a 40% open rate. What does that tell you? What would you change?
3. How do you balance being informative with being too long? (Answer: 3-5 sections, each 2-3 sentences. Parents skim; put the most important item first.)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Sending newsletters on random days | Parents never know when to expect it; engagement drops | Every Friday by 3 PM. No exceptions. Consistency builds the habit. |
| Writing paragraphs | Nobody reads a wall of text | 2-3 sentences per section. Use headers. Parents skim. |
| Forgetting photos | Newsletter feels generic and institutional | Include at least 2 photos per newsletter |
| Not using announcements for closures | Parents miss critical info buried in a newsletter | Closures and emergencies get announcements (push + email), not newsletters |
| Skipping newsletters during slow weeks | Parents assume you forgot about them | There's always something to share. Even "this week was calm and productive" is a message. |

### Handout Content: Weekly Newsletter Template

**Christina's Weekly Update: [Date]**

1. **This Week's Highlight:** [1-2 sentences + 1 photo]
2. **Classroom Spotlight:** [Brief note from one classroom + 1 photo]
3. **Coming Up Next Week:** [3-5 bullet points of events, deadlines, reminders]
4. **Menu Heads-Up:** [Any special meals or allergy notes for next week]
5. **Thank You:** [Shout out to a parent volunteer, a staff member, or a child milestone]

Send every Friday by 3 PM. Use scheduling to write it Thursday and auto-send Friday.

## M12: Parent Portal Mastery

**Format:** Self-paced (15 min) + paper welcome packet
**Roles:** Parents (Tier 1)

This module walks parents through everything they see in their portal. The goal: parents should know exactly where to find information about their child, and they should understand that what they enter directly affects what teachers and directors see.

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Print the parent welcome packet (see Handout Content)
- Have a sample parent account logged in on a large screen
- Walk through every section of the parent portal while narrating
- Stress the allergy and emergency contact sections (this is compliance and safety)

**What to demo:**
- Open /dashboard and walk through every card and section
- Show what happens when a parent updates their child's allergy info (it changes what the teacher sees immediately)
- Show the photo gallery, daily reports, and messages
- Demonstrate the document section for signed forms and policies
- Show the notification preferences and explain what each notification means

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough: Every Section a Parent Sees

**1. Dashboard Home (/dashboard)**

Your dashboard is the first thing you see when you log in. It shows:
- **Today's summary card:** Your child's check-in time, meals served, activities, and any notes from the teacher
- **Alerts banner:** Anything that needs your attention (missing forms, upcoming events, unread messages)
- **Quick actions:** Buttons for messaging, viewing photos, and checking the calendar

[Screenshot: Parent dashboard home page showing summary card and alerts]

**2. My Children (/dashboard/children)**

This is where you manage your child's complete profile. Each section matters.

- **Basic Info:** Name, date of birth, gender. Make sure the legal name matches enrollment documents.
- **Allergies:** This section is critical. When you add or update an allergy here, your child's teacher sees it immediately on their classroom roster. If you add "peanut allergy, severe" today, every staff member who works with your child can see it by tomorrow morning. Do not wait to update this.
- **Medical Info:** Medications, doctor's name and phone, insurance. If your child takes medication during the day, this section must be complete before the medication can be administered.
- **Emergency Contacts:** List at least 2 people (besides yourself) who can be called in an emergency and who are authorized to pick up your child. Include: full name, relationship, phone number, and whether they are authorized for pickup.
- **Authorized Pickup:** Every person who might ever pick up your child. Staff will check this list at the door. If someone arrives who is not listed, they will be turned away, even if they say you sent them. This is a licensing requirement, not a preference.
- **Dietary Preferences:** Vegetarian, kosher, halal, or other dietary needs for meal planning.

**What your child's teacher sees when you update this:**

When you change your child's profile, the update flows through the system:
- Allergy updates appear on the teacher's classroom roster and daily report view within minutes
- Emergency contact changes are visible on the teacher's quick-reference card for your child
- Dietary preference updates affect meal planning and the kitchen's prep list
- Authorized pickup changes are visible at the front desk and kiosk

Teachers do not need to be told separately. The system carries the update to everyone who needs it. But the update only works if you make it. Outdated information is worse than no information, because staff will trust what the system says.

- Browse photos from your child's classroom, organized by date
- Each photo shows the activity tag (Art, Outdoor Play, etc.) and a teacher caption
- You can save photos to your phone
- Photos are uploaded by teachers and approved by the director before you see them

- Send and receive messages with your child's teacher and the director
- Full conversation history is preserved
- Response expectation: within 4 hours during business hours
- Use for: schedule questions, quick updates, general check-ins
- Do not use for: emergencies (call the center directly)

**5. Calendar (/dashboard/calendar)**

- Center events, closures, and holidays
- Parent-teacher conference dates

- Weekly newsletters from the director

- All your alerts in one place
- Unread notifications show a badge count
- Types: new photos, messages, newsletters, incident reports, schedule changes
- Configure which notifications you receive (covered in M13)

**8. Documents (/dashboard/documents)**

- Signed enrollment forms
- Center policies and handbooks

### Discussion Questions

1. You discover your child developed a new food allergy over the weekend. Where do you update this, and how quickly should you do it? (Answer: /dashboard/children, allergy section, immediately on the same day you learn about it, before the next school day)
2. Your mother-in-law is picking up your child on Thursday. She is not on the authorized pickup list. What needs to happen before Thursday? (Answer: add her to authorized pickup on /dashboard/children before she arrives)
3. You notice the emergency contact phone number for your spouse is their old number. What is the risk of not updating it? (Answer: in an emergency, staff calls a disconnected number; your child waits longer for help)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Treating allergy info as optional | Staff may serve food that triggers a reaction | Fill this out before your child's first day, period |
| Only listing yourself as emergency contact | If you can't be reached, nobody else can be called | At least 2 additional contacts besides yourself |
| Not checking the portal for a week | You miss photos, updates, and important announcements | Check 2-3 times per week; Monday, Wednesday, Friday is a good rhythm |
| Assuming the center already knows about changes | Teachers rely on the system; verbal updates get forgotten | If it changes in your life, update it in the portal |
| Not reviewing documents when prompted | Forms sit unsigned; this delays enrollment or re-enrollment | When you see a document alert, review and sign that day |

## M13: Notification Management

**Format:** Self-paced (5 min)
**Roles:** Parents (Tier 1), Employees (Tier 2)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Know where notification preferences live in each portal
- Prepare a list of notification types and which ones are mandatory vs. optional
- Have a sample push notification and email notification to show side by side

**What to demo:**
- Open notification preferences on a parent account and an employee account
- Show the toggle switches for each notification type
- Explain which notifications are mandatory (incident reports, emergency closures) and cannot be turned off
- Show the difference between push notifications (phone alert) and email notifications
- Demonstrate the weekly digest option (one summary email instead of many individual ones)

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**Parent view (/dashboard/notifications):**

1. Go to /dashboard/notifications
2. At the top, you see your unread notifications (badge count on the nav shows how many)
3. Scroll down or tap "Preferences" to manage settings
4. Notification types you can configure:
   - **New Photos:** Get notified when new photos are approved for your child's classroom
   - **Messages:** Get notified when a teacher or director sends you a message
   - **Newsletters:** Get notified when a new newsletter is published
   - **Daily Reports:** Get a summary of your child's day at a set time
   - **Calendar Events:** Reminders for upcoming events and closures
   - **Document Requests:** Alert when a new form needs your signature
5. Notifications you cannot turn off:
   - **Incident Reports:** If your child is involved in an incident, you will always be notified
   - **Emergency Closures:** Weather, power outage, or safety closures
   - **Schedule Changes:** If the center's hours change for any reason
6. For each notification type, choose delivery method:
   - **Push:** Alert on your phone (requires browser notifications enabled)
   - **Email:** Sent to your registered email
   - **Both:** Recommended for critical items

**Employee view:**

1. Go to /employee/profile or the notification settings section
2. Configure notifications for:
   - **Task Assignments:** When a new task is assigned to you
   - **Schedule Changes:** When your shift is modified
   - **Messages:** When a parent or director messages you
   - **Incident Alerts:** When an incident is filed in your classroom
   - **Certification Expiry:** 90/60/30 day reminders before a cert expires
   - **Training Assignments:** When new training modules are assigned

### Discussion Questions

1. You are a parent who turned off photo notifications because you "get too many." What might you miss? (Answer: you miss seeing your child's day, which is one of the main engagement features)
2. You are an employee who turned off task assignment notifications. What happens when a director assigns you an urgent task? (Answer: you don't see it until you happen to open the app; the task may go overdue)
3. What is the minimum set of notifications every parent should keep on? (Answer: messages, incident reports, emergency closures, and schedule changes)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Turning off all notifications | You miss critical info about your child or your job | Keep mandatory notifications on; adjust the optional ones |
| Not enabling push notifications in your browser | You never see alerts even if they are turned on in the platform | When prompted to "Allow Notifications," say yes |
| Ignoring the weekly digest | Important items pile up unread | Pick one day per week to read the digest (Sunday evening works well) |
| Having email notifications go to spam | You never see them | Add the center's email to your contacts or whitelist it |

### Handout Content: Notification Setup Card

**Set Up Your Notifications**

1. Log in to your portal
2. Go to notification preferences (Settings or Profile section)
3. Turn ON these notifications (minimum):
   - Messages: ON (push + email)
   - Incident Reports: ON (always on, cannot be turned off)
   - Emergency Closures: ON (always on, cannot be turned off)
   - Schedule Changes: ON (always on, cannot be turned off)
4. Recommended ON:
   - Photos: ON (push)
   - Daily Reports: ON (email)
   - Newsletters: ON (email)
5. When your phone asks "Allow Notifications?": tap YES

## Unit 3 Summary

After completing this unit, every participant should be able to:

1. Send and receive messages with a 4-hour response window (parents and staff)
2. Create and send a weekly newsletter with photos and sections (directors/owner)
3. Navigate every section of the parent portal and keep all child info current (parents)
4. Configure notifications so critical alerts always come through (all roles)

**The communication rhythm:**
- Daily: Check and respond to messages
- Weekly: Director sends Friday newsletter; parents open and read it
- Ongoing: Parents update child profiles when anything changes
- Monthly: Director reviews read receipts and adjusts communication strategy
