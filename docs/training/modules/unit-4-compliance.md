# Unit 4: Compliance & Safety

**Modules:** M14 CACFP Compliance Deep Dive, M15 Ratio Monitoring & Compliance, M16 Incident Reporting, M17 Certifications & Training Tracking
**Audience:** Employees (Tier 2), Directors (Tier 3), Owner (Tier 4)
**Total Time:** 2.5-3 hours (facilitated PD sessions + self-paced)

This is the unit that keeps the license on the wall. Every module here connects to state licensing requirements, federal program compliance, or child safety protocols. Mistakes in this unit are not workflow inconveniences. They are fines, suspended reimbursements, licensing citations, and in the worst cases, closed centers. Take this unit seriously.

## M14: CACFP Compliance Deep Dive

**Format:** Facilitated PD session (60 min) with self-assessment
**Roles:** Directors (Tier 3), Owner (Tier 4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Pull up the /admin/compliance dashboard on a large screen
- Prepare a sample audit scenario (see Assessment below)
- Print the CACFP compliance checklist (see Handout Content)
- Have the current federal reimbursement rate sheet available
- Calculate your center's annual CACFP revenue so the stakes feel real
- Review the center's current audit readiness score before the session

**What to demo:**
- Walk through every section of the /admin/compliance dashboard
- Show how the audit readiness score is calculated (what counts, what doesn't)
- Demonstrate generating a compliance report for a specific date range
- Show what a "green" center looks like vs. a "yellow" center vs. a "red" center
- Walk through the connection between daily meal counts (M06) and the compliance score
- Show the reimbursement calculation: how many dollars each classroom generates per month

**Key context:** CACFP is the Child and Adult Care Food Program, a federal program administered through your state agency. It reimburses child care centers for serving nutritious meals to enrolled children. Your center's CACFP reimbursement likely falls between $40,000 and $80,000 per year. An audit failure can suspend this reimbursement entirely, meaning you would need to cover all food costs out of pocket while the suspension is active. Some centers never recover financially from a suspension.

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**The Compliance Dashboard (/admin/compliance):**

1. Go to /admin/compliance
2. The top section shows your overall **Audit Readiness Score** (0-100%)
   - **90-100% (Green):** You are audit-ready. Maintain this.
   - **75-89% (Yellow):** You have gaps. Address them this week.
   - **Below 75% (Red):** You are at risk of audit failure. Address immediately.
3. Below the score, you see component breakdowns:
   - **Meal Count Accuracy:** Are daily counts submitted on time with correct numbers?
   - **Attendance Alignment:** Do meal counts match attendance records? (A child marked absent cannot have a meal count.)
   - **Menu Compliance:** Do served meals meet CACFP nutritional requirements?
   - **Documentation Completeness:** Are enrollment forms, income eligibility forms, and meal production records current?
   - **Staff Training:** Have all staff completed required CACFP training?
4. Each component is color-coded. Tap any component to see the specific items that need attention.
5. The **Timeline View** shows your score trend over weeks and months. Look for patterns: does your score dip every Friday? That means end-of-week meal counts are being missed.

**Generating Compliance Reports:**

1. From /admin/compliance, tap "Generate Report"
2. Select the date range (weekly, monthly, or custom)
3. The report includes:
   - Meal count submission rates by classroom
   - Attendance-to-meal-count alignment percentage
   - Missing or incomplete records
   - Estimated reimbursement amount for the period
   - Items flagged for corrective action
4. Export as PDF for your records or for sharing with your CACFP sponsor

**Understanding Reimbursement Calculations:**

1. From /admin/compliance or /admin/food-counts, find the reimbursement section
2. The platform calculates reimbursement based on:
   - Number of children served each meal
   - Income eligibility tier of each child (Free, Reduced, Paid)
   - Current federal reimbursement rates
3. Example: 30 children x lunch x Free tier rate ($1.40) = $42/day for lunch alone
4. Multiply across all meals and all days in the month for your projected reimbursement
5. Compare projected vs. actual to identify where money is being left on the table

### Discussion Questions

1. Your audit readiness score dropped from 92% to 78% in two weeks. What are the first three things you check?
2. A CACFP reviewer calls and says they will visit next Thursday. What do you do between now and then?
3. Why is the attendance-to-meal-count alignment so important? (Answer: if a child is marked absent but has a meal count, that is fraud. If a child is present but has no meal count, that is lost revenue. Either way, you lose.)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Only checking compliance before an audit | Problems pile up invisibly for months | Check the compliance dashboard every Monday morning |
| Ignoring yellow-flagged items | Yellow becomes red; red becomes audit failure | Address every yellow item within the same week |
| Not training substitutes on CACFP procedures | Subs miss meal counts or submit incorrect numbers | Include CACFP basics in every substitute orientation |
| Assuming attendance and meal counts match automatically | They only match if everyone does their job correctly | Run the alignment check weekly |
| Not saving compliance reports | You have no evidence of your ongoing readiness | Generate and save a monthly report every month |

## M15: Ratio Monitoring & Compliance

**Format:** Self-paced (15 min) + facilitated scenario session (30 min)
**Roles:** Employees (Tier 2), Directors (Tier 3), Owner (Tier 4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Post your state's staff-to-child ratio requirements on the wall or screen
- Pull up /admin/ratios on a large screen
- Prepare the "two staff called in sick" scenario (see Assessment below)
- Know the difference between a ratio that is tight but compliant vs. an actual violation

**What to demo:**
- Show the /admin/ratios dashboard with real-time classroom data
- Demonstrate what happens when a ratio goes from green to yellow to red
- Walk through the age-group-specific requirements (infants, toddlers, preschool, school-age)
- Show how attendance changes throughout the day (drop-off, nap time, pickup) affect ratios
- Demonstrate the alert system when a ratio violation is detected

**Key context:** Staff-to-child ratios are set by your state licensing agency. They are not suggestions. A single documented ratio violation during a licensing inspection can result in a $500-5,000 fine, a corrective action plan, or in severe cases, license suspension. The platform monitors ratios in real time so you can act before a violation happens, not after.

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**Employee view (awareness, not management):**

Employees need to know when their room is approaching a ratio concern, even if they are not the ones who fix it.

1. Your employee dashboard may show a ratio indicator for your classroom
2. If the indicator is green: you are within ratio. Continue normal operations.
3. If the indicator is yellow: your room is getting close to the limit. Be aware.
4. If the indicator is red: you are at or over ratio. Notify the director immediately. Do not leave the room.

**What to do when your room is over ratio:**
- Stay in the room. Do not leave for any reason.
- Contact the director (message, walkie, or send another staff member to find them)
- The director will either move a child to another room or send another staff member to your room
- Do not attempt to move children yourself without director approval

**Director/Owner view (/admin/ratios):**

1. Go to /admin/ratios
2. The dashboard shows every classroom as a card:
   - Classroom name and age group
   - Number of children present
   - Number of staff assigned and present
   - Current ratio (e.g., 1:4 for infants, 1:7 for toddlers, 1:10 for preschool)
   - Required ratio per state regulation
   - Status color: green (good), yellow (within 1 child of limit), red (at or over limit)
3. The **Projection View** shows how ratios will change throughout the day based on scheduled arrivals, departures, and staff shift changes
4. Alerts trigger automatically when:
   - A room reaches 90% of its ratio capacity (yellow)
   - A room meets or exceeds its ratio limit (red)
   - A staff member clocks out or is marked absent, changing the ratio

**State ratio requirements (Minnesota example):**

| Age Group | Required Ratio | Max Group Size |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Infants (6 weeks - 16 months) | 1:4 | 8 |
| Toddlers (16-33 months) | 1:7 | 14 |
| Preschool (33 months - kindergarten) | 1:10 | 20 |
| School-age (kindergarten+) | 1:15 | 30 |

*Verify these against your current state licensing rules. Ratios vary by state and may change.*

### Discussion Questions

1. It is 2:30 PM. One of your two preschool teachers just left for the day. You have 18 preschool children present. What is your ratio? Are you in compliance? What do you do? (Answer: 1:18 with one teacher. Required ratio is 1:10. You need at least 2 teachers. Send help immediately.)
2. A parent drops off unexpectedly at 10 AM with a child who wasn't on today's attendance list. Does this affect your ratios? (Answer: Yes. One additional child could push a room to its limit. Check ratios before placing the child.)
3. During nap time, can one teacher leave the room while another watches all 14 toddlers? (Answer: No. Ratios apply during nap time. 1:7 means you need 2 staff for 14 toddlers, even if they are sleeping.)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Assuming nap time doesn't count | A licensing inspector can walk in during nap; ratios still apply | Ratios are 24/7 while children are present |
| Not accounting for staff breaks | Staff member goes on break; room drops a teacher; ratio violated | Stagger breaks so coverage is maintained |
| Ignoring the yellow warning | Yellow turns red quickly during transitions (arrival, departure) | Address yellow immediately; don't wait for red |
| Moving children between rooms without telling the director | Ratio tracking becomes inaccurate | All room moves go through the director |
| Relying on memory instead of the dashboard | "I think we're fine" is not documentation | Check /admin/ratios at every transition point |

### Handout Content: Ratio Quick Reference

**Post this in every classroom at adult eye level.**

| My Classroom | Age Group | Required Ratio | With 1 Staff | With 2 Staff | With 3 Staff |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Infant Room | 6 wks-16 mo | 1:4 | 4 max | 8 max | 12 max |
| Toddler Room | 16-33 mo | 1:7 | 7 max | 14 max | 21 max |
| Preschool Room | 33 mo-K | 1:10 | 10 max | 20 max | 30 max |

**If your room is at the limit:**
1. Do NOT leave the room
2. Contact the director immediately
3. Wait for help to arrive or a child to be moved

**Check ratios at:** Arrival, mid-morning, lunch, nap, departure, and any time a staff member leaves.

## M16: Incident Reporting

**Format:** Facilitated session (45 min) with practice scenarios
**Roles:** Employees (Tier 2), Directors (Tier 3), Owner (Tier 4)

This module covers the most serious process in the center. When a child is hurt, becomes ill, has a behavioral incident, or experiences anything that affects their safety, the incident must be documented correctly, reported promptly, and tracked through resolution. There is no room for "I'll get to it later." Thirty minutes. That is the window for filing.

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Have the /admin/incidents page open on a large screen
- Create a sample practice incident in the system (already resolved) so participants can see all fields
- Print the severity level decision tree (see below)
- Print the parent notification protocol
- Review your state's licensure-reportable incident criteria before the session
- Prepare 3 practice scenarios at different severity levels

**What to demo:**
- File a complete incident report step by step
- Show every field and explain why it exists
- Demonstrate the severity level selector and walk through the decision tree
- Show the parent notification workflow (how parents are notified through the system)
- Show the audit trail (who created the report, when it was edited, by whom, what changed)
- Show the incident log (/admin/incidents/log) and how past incidents are stored permanently
- Show the training connection (/admin/incidents/training) for follow-up when incidents reveal a training gap

### Full Walkthrough: Filing an Incident Report

**Step 1: Open the incident form**

1. Go to /admin/incidents (or the incident section from /employee if available)
2. Tap "File New Incident"

**Step 2: Identify the child/children involved**

3. Select the child(ren) from the dropdown (connected to your enrollment data)
4. If multiple children are involved, select all of them

**Step 3: Record the basics**

5. **Date and Time:** When did the incident happen? (Not when you are filing the report; when the actual incident occurred.)
6. **Location:** Where in the center? (Classroom name, playground, hallway, bathroom, parking lot)
7. **Staff Present:** Who was in the room at the time? (Select from staff roster)
8. **Witnesses:** Any other adults present? (Parents, volunteers, visitors)

**Step 4: Describe what happened**

9. **Incident Description:** Write a factual, chronological account. Stick to what you observed.
   - Good: "At 10:15 AM, Jordan fell from the second step of the climber in the outdoor play area. He landed on his left side on the rubber surface. He cried immediately. I was standing 4 feet away and reached him within 3 seconds."
   - Bad: "Jordan fell off something outside. He was fine."
10. **Injuries Observed:** Describe any visible injuries. Location, size, color. "Red mark on left forearm, approximately 1 inch, no swelling."
11. **Action Taken:** What did you do? First aid, ice pack, comfort, called parent, called 911?

**Step 5: Assign severity level**

12. Select the severity level using the decision tree below.

### Severity Level Decision Tree

Ask these questions in order. The first "yes" determines the severity level.

**Level 4 (Critical):**
- Did the child lose consciousness?
- Was 911 called?
- Does the child need emergency medical care (broken bone, head injury with altered behavior, severe allergic reaction, breathing difficulty)?
- Is this a suspected abuse or neglect situation?

If YES to any: **Level 4. File immediately. Notify director immediately. Notify parent immediately. This may be licensure-reportable.**

**Level 3 (Serious):**
- Does the child need medical attention beyond basic first aid (doctor visit, urgent care)?
- Is there a bite that broke the skin?
- Is there a significant visible injury (large bruise, deep cut, swelling)?
- Did the child leave the supervised area (elopement)?

If YES to any: **Level 3. File within 30 minutes. Notify director within 15 minutes. Notify parent within 1 hour.**

**Level 2 (Moderate):**
- Did the child receive basic first aid (ice pack, bandaid, cleaning a scrape)?
- Was there a conflict between children that required staff intervention?
- Did the child have an emotional episode requiring extended comfort?

If YES to any: **Level 2. File within 30 minutes. Notify parent by end of day.**

**Level 1 (Minor):**
- Minor bump or scrape, no first aid needed
- Brief conflict resolved quickly with no injury
- Child was upset but recovered within a few minutes

If YES: **Level 1. File by end of shift. Notify parent at pickup.**

### Parent Notification Protocol

The platform sends parent notifications based on severity level, but staff must also communicate directly.

| Severity | System Notification | Direct Communication |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Level 4 (Critical) | Immediate push + email | Phone call to parent immediately |
| Level 3 (Serious) | Within 1 hour of filing | Phone call to parent within 1 hour |
| Level 2 (Moderate) | Same-day notification | In-person at pickup or phone call |
| Level 1 (Minor) | Daily summary or next message | Mention at pickup |

**What the parent notification includes:**
- Date, time, and brief description of the incident
- Action taken
- Current status of the child
- Contact information for follow-up questions

**What the parent notification does NOT include:**
- Names of other children involved (privacy)
- Staff opinions or speculation about cause
- Information that should be communicated by the director in person

### Audit Trail Explanation

Every incident report in the system has a complete audit trail. Here is why this matters and what it means.

**What is tracked:**
- Who created the report and when (timestamp to the second)
- Every edit: who changed what, when, and what the previous value was
- Parent notification: when it was sent, when it was opened
- Follow-up actions: who was assigned, completion status
- Resolution: when the incident was marked resolved, by whom

**Why every edit is tracked:**
- Legal protection: If a parent or attorney requests records, you have a complete, timestamped history
- Licensing protection: Inspectors can see that you documented promptly and followed up
- Integrity: Nobody can quietly change a report after the fact without the change being recorded
- Pattern detection: The system can identify repeated incidents (same child, same location, same time of day) to reveal safety issues

**What this means for staff:**
- File the report honestly the first time. Mistakes happen; note them and correct the record. But the original entry is always preserved.
- Do not ask someone else to file a report for you unless you physically cannot (injured, no phone access). Your name should be on the reports you file.
- Do not edit a report to remove information. If something was recorded incorrectly, add a note explaining the correction. The original text remains in the audit trail.

### Licensure-Reportable Criteria

Certain incidents must be reported to your state licensing agency, not just documented internally. The platform flags potential reportable incidents, but the final determination is the director's or owner's responsibility.

**Generally reportable incidents in Minnesota (verify with your current licensing rules):**

1. **Death of a child while in care** (report immediately; call 911 first)
2. **Serious injury requiring hospitalization** (report within 24 hours)
3. **Suspected abuse or neglect** (mandated reporting; call county intake within 24 hours)
4. **Child left unsupervised** (elopement, forgotten on bus, locked in room)
5. **Unauthorized person attempted or completed pickup**
6. **Medication error** (wrong child, wrong dose, wrong medication)
7. **Fire, natural disaster, or structural emergency** requiring evacuation
8. **Any incident resulting in a police response**

**What to do when an incident is reportable:**
1. File the report in the system at Level 3 or Level 4
2. Notify the director/owner immediately
3. The director/owner contacts the licensing agency within the required timeframe
4. Document the licensing report in the system (date, who was contacted, reference number)
5. Cooperate fully with any licensing investigation
6. Save all records; do not delete or alter anything

### Discussion Questions

1. A child gets a small scrape on their knee during outdoor play. No bleeding, no tears, the child ran right back to playing. Do you file a report? (Answer: Level 1, yes. Document everything. A parent who sees the scrape at pickup will ask what happened, and you need a record.)
2. During nap time, you notice a bruise on a child's arm that was not there at drop-off and did not happen at the center. What do you do? (Answer: This may be a mandated reporting situation. Document what you observe, notify the director immediately. Do not question the child or the parent. Follow your mandated reporter protocol.)
3. You filed a report but realized you got the time wrong. Can you change it? (Answer: Yes, edit the report and correct the time. The original timestamp and the correction are both preserved in the audit trail. Add a note: "Time corrected from 10:15 to 10:45; original entry was based on memory, corrected after reviewing classroom clock.")

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Waiting until end of day to file | Details are fuzzy; timestamps are inaccurate; you look negligent | File within 30 minutes of the incident. Period. |
| Writing vague descriptions | "He fell" tells nobody anything useful | Answer who, what, where, when, how. Be specific. |
| Guessing the severity level | Under-severity means parents aren't notified properly; over-severity creates unnecessary alarm | Use the decision tree every time |
| Not notifying parents for Level 2+ | Parent finds out about the injury at pickup from their child; trust is destroyed | Follow the notification protocol for every severity level |
| Editing reports to look better after the fact | Audit trail shows the change; this looks like a cover-up | File honestly the first time. Correct errors with notes, not deletions. |
| Not connecting repeated incidents to a pattern | The same child keeps getting hurt; nobody investigates why | Review the incident log monthly for patterns |

## M17: Certifications & Training Tracking

**Format:** Self-paced (10 min)
**Roles:** Employees (Tier 2), Directors (Tier 3), Owner (Tier 4)

### Facilitator Notes

**Before the session:**
- Have each participant's certification status loaded in the system
- Know the required certifications for your state (CPR/First Aid, background check, annual training hours)
- Prepare an example of what a 90-day, 60-day, and 30-day expiry alert looks like
- Know the cost and time impact of an expired certification (the employee cannot legally work in ratio)

**What to demo:**
- Show the employee certification page on /employee/profile or /employee/training

- Demonstrate the expiry alert system (90/60/30 day warnings)

### Step-by-Step Platform Walkthrough

**Employee view (/employee/training and /employee/profile):**

1. Go to /employee/training or /employee/profile
2. Find the "Certifications" section
3. You see a list of your certifications:
   - Certification name (CPR, First Aid, Background Check, Annual Training Hours, etc.)
   - Status: Current, Expiring Soon, Expired
   - Expiry date
   - Days remaining (color-coded: green = 90+ days, yellow = 30-89 days, red = under 30 days)
4. To upload a renewed certification:
   - Tap the certification
   - Tap "Upload New Certificate"
   - Take a photo of the certificate or select a file
   - Enter the new expiry date
   - Tap "Submit"
   - The director will verify and approve the update
5. Your training hours are also tracked here:
   - Required annual hours (state mandate)
   - Hours completed this year
   - Training modules completed (including this curriculum)

**Director/Owner view (/admin/staff/development):**

1. Go to /admin/staff/development
2. The certification dashboard shows all employees:
   - Name, role, and classroom assignment
   - Certification status for each required cert
   - Overall compliance: green (all current), yellow (expiring soon), red (expired or missing)
3. The **Expiring Soon** panel shows certifications coming due in the next 90 days
4. The **Expired** panel shows certifications that have lapsed (these employees may not be able to work in ratio until renewed)
5. Tap any employee to see their full certification record
6. Use this view to:
   - Plan training days (schedule CPR renewals before they expire)
   - Budget for certification costs
   - Assign training modules from the curriculum

**The alert system:**

| 90 days | First notice | Employee + Director |
| 60 days | Second notice | Employee + Director |
| 30 days | Urgent notice | Employee + Director + Owner |
| 0 days (expired) | Expired alert | Employee + Director + Owner |

After expiration, the employee's name appears in red on the scheduling tool. This is a flag for the director: this person cannot be counted toward ratio until their certification is renewed.

### Discussion Questions

1. Your CPR certification expires in 45 days. When should you schedule the renewal class? (Answer: now. Don't wait for the 30-day alert. Renewal classes fill up, and if you miss the window, you can't work in ratio.)
2. A staff member's background check expired last week. Can they still work? (Answer: this depends on state law, but generally: they should not be counted in ratio and may not be allowed in unsupervised contact with children until renewed. Check your licensing requirements.)
3. Who is responsible for tracking certifications: the employee or the director? (Answer: both. The employee is responsible for renewing. The director is responsible for monitoring and not scheduling someone whose certification has lapsed.)

### Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | What Happens | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Ignoring the 90-day alert | 90 becomes 60, becomes 30, becomes expired | Schedule renewal at the first alert |
| Not uploading the renewed cert | The system still shows it as expired even though you renewed | Upload the new certificate the same day you receive it |
| Scheduling an employee with an expired cert | Licensing violation if inspected | Check /admin/staff/development before publishing the weekly schedule |
| Not budgeting for renewal costs | Surprise expenses; employees pay out of pocket and resent it | Budget certification costs annually; the dashboard shows upcoming renewals |
| Relying on employees to self-report | Some forget; some don't know their expiry date | The system tracks it. Trust the system, not memory. |
